Kimberly

Kimberly is the capital city of the Northern Cape province of South Africa, also known as the diamond city after diamonds were first discovered here in 1871. The city’s world-class tourist destination, the Big Hole, is the deepest man-made crater in the world, and the size of eight football fields. Visitors can also marvel at the bygone and characteristic architecture in Kimberly’s oldest suburb, Belgravia, dating back to the 1870s. The historic suburb of Belgravia hosts 33 of the Northern Cape's most historical sites.
